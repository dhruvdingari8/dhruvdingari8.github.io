Projects

Battery Management System

The Battery Management System is a long term project of the Fixed Wing Project of AerospaceNU, of which I am the electrical and software lead for. The need for the BMS came from being a former Capstone project designed to provide a variable cell balancing and monitoring solution for drones, specifically aimed to manage 3-12 Li-on battery cells. A custom 6 layer PCB was designed, printed and assembled, where I took ownership of the design of the 5V regulator in use on the board. The design converts anywhere from 6V to 60V DC voltage from the battery pack, and regulates it to a steady 5V 10A output power to power the motors and peripherals of the drone. I also wrote and designed C firmware to connect the RP2040 MCU chip to the BQ76972 Battery Stack Monitor IC being used to manage the cells. The RP2040 communicates to the BQ IC over SPI to read individual cell voltages, thermistor temperature readings, and other parameters. Right now, the goal is to use this BMS to manage the power on the drone being designed for competing in the University Rover Competition.

LineVision Power Consumption Testing

During my Co-op at LineVision, I had the opportunity to work on the upcoming new sensor platform. Specifically, I owned the test plan and design for the power consumption test of the device. The goal of the test was to measure the power draw of the device over the course of 50 hours, and how long the power duty cycle could be. For this project, I used Python to connect to a Keysight DMM and continuously poll it for current draw. From that, I used Numpy and Pandas to calculate and display the average power draw neatly on a graph. Using the average values calculated from this, I created an algorithm used to determine what the power draw would be over a 24 hour period, given certain duty cycles (i.e. on for 30 seconds out of 10 minute intervals). With the maximum duty cycle lengths, the company could move forward with deciding on a specific weather station to use for the eventual final product, as well as firmware limitations on how long specific processes could take per cycle.

  • Data Visualization with Matplotlib
